Longganisang Kapampangan

Sausage Sweet Breakfast

What is Longganisa?

Longganisa is a Filipino-style sausage. The Kapampangan version is known for being sweet and garlicky with a reddish color from annatto. It is one of the most famous regional sausages in the Philippines and a beloved breakfast food.

Kapampangan longganisa is usually small and stubby. It is pan-fried until the skin caramelizes and becomes slightly crispy. It is served with garlic fried rice and a fried egg — a combination called longsilog.

What Makes It Special?

Every region in the Philippines has its own longganisa, but Kapampangan longganisa stands out because:

  • It has a notably sweet flavor from sugar
  • It uses a lot of garlic for a bold, aromatic taste
  • The annatto seeds give it a beautiful orange-red color
  • It uses a high ratio of fat to meat, making it rich and juicy
  • Some versions use vinegar for a slight sour note that balances the sweetness

Kapampangan Longganisa Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 500g ground pork (with fat)
  • 1 head garlic, minced very fine
  • 3 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon annatto powder (or liquid)
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Pork casings (cleaned)

Instructions:

  1. Mix all ingredients together with the ground pork.
  2. Let the mixture marinate overnight in the refrigerator.
  3. Stuff the mixture into pork casings and tie into small links.
  4. Let dry for a few hours or overnight.
  5. Pan-fry in a little water first, then in oil until caramelized.
  6. Serve with garlic rice and fried egg.
