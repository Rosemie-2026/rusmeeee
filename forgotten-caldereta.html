Calderetang Kambing

Rare Goat Stew Spanish Influence

What is Calderetang Kambing?

Calderetang Kambing is a goat meat stew cooked in liver sauce, tomatoes, and bell peppers, with olives and potatoes added. It is the goat version of the popular Caldereta stew, which is usually made with beef or pork today.

In the past, goats were commonly raised in Pampanga. They were slaughtered for special occasions, and Calderetang Kambing was the celebratory dish made from the goat meat. It has a rich, gamey flavor from the goat meat combined with the thick, savory liver sauce.

The Problem: Goats Are Gone

Goat farming in Pampanga has declined greatly over the past few decades. As the province became more urbanized, open land for raising goats disappeared. Goat meat became expensive and hard to find. As a result, this dish became rarer and rarer until it almost completely disappeared from Kapampangan tables.

Calderetang Kambing Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg goat meat, cut into serving pieces
  • 3 tablespoons liver spread or mashed goat liver
  • 1 can tomato sauce
  • 1 cup beef or goat broth
  • 2 medium potatoes, cubed
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1/2 cup green olives
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 2 tablespoons vinegar
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Marinate goat meat in vinegar, salt, and pepper for at least 1 hour.
  2. Sauté garlic and onion. Add goat meat and brown on all sides.
  3. Add tomato sauce and broth. Simmer for 45 minutes until tender.
  4. Add potatoes and cook for 10 minutes.
  5. Stir in liver spread. Add bell peppers and olives.
  6. Season with salt. Simmer for 5 more minutes and serve.

How to Help

If you can find goat meat at a local market, trying this dish is a great way to honor the Kapampangan tradition. Some wet markets in Pampanga still occasionally sell goat meat on special weekends.
