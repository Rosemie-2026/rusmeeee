Sisig

Pork Sizzling Plate Angeles City

What is Sisig?

Sisig is one of the most famous Filipino dishes in the world, and it was invented in Angeles City, Pampanga. It is made from chopped pork cheeks, ears, and sometimes liver, served sizzling hot on a cast iron plate.

The dish is seasoned with calamansi juice, onions, chili peppers, and sometimes mayonnaise or egg. It is usually eaten as a main dish with rice or as a beer match (pulutan).

Who Invented Sisig?

Sisig was invented by Lucia "Aling Lucing" Cunanan in the 1970s. She had a small roadside restaurant along MacArthur Highway in Angeles City. She used pig face parts (ears, cheeks, snout) that American soldiers threw away from Clark Air Base. She boiled them, grilled them, and then chopped them with onions and calamansi.

Over the years, the recipe evolved into the sizzling plate version we know today. Aling Lucing was given the title "Queen of Sisig" before she passed away.

Basic Sisig Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 500g pork cheeks or pork face
  • 100g chicken liver
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 pieces calamansi
  • 2 pieces chili peppers
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon mayonnaise (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Boil the pork face until tender. Let it cool.
  2. Grill or broil the boiled pork until slightly charred.
  3. Chop the pork into small pieces.
  4. Sauté garlic and onion in a hot pan.
  5. Add the chopped pork and liver. Season with soy sauce.
  6. Squeeze calamansi juice over the top.
  7. Serve on a sizzling hot plate with chili on top.

Fun Facts About Sisig

  • Sisig was added to the Oxford English Dictionary in 2021.
  • There is an annual Sisig Festival in Angeles City, Pampanga.
  • Sisig has many modern versions: seafood sisig, tofu sisig, and even bangus (milkfish) sisig.
  • The word "sisig" originally referred to a sour marinade in old Kapampangan.
