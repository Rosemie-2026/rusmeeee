About Kapampangan Cuisine

Pampanga, a province in Central Luzon, Philippines, is widely considered the Culinary Capital of the country. The people of Pampanga, called Kapampangans, have a deep passion for cooking and eating good food.

Who Are the Kapampangans?

The Kapampangans are an ethnic group native to the province of Pampanga. They speak the Kapampangan language, also called Capampangan or Pampangueño. They are known throughout the Philippines for their exceptional cooking skills.

Kapampangan women, in particular, were historically recognized as the best cooks in the Philippines. Many of the country's most popular dishes originated from Pampanga.

Why Is Their Food Special?

  • They use fresh, local ingredients sourced from Pampanga's rich farmland.
  • Their cooking combines indigenous, Spanish, Chinese, and American influences.
  • They have a tradition of slow cooking and fermenting foods for deep flavor.
  • They are known for their use of pork, offal, and fermented shrimp paste (bagoong).
  • Their desserts use carabao milk, which gives a unique creamy taste.

The Problem: Forgotten Dishes

Many traditional Kapampangan dishes are disappearing. Younger generations are moving to cities, eating fast food, and losing touch with traditional cooking. Some recipes only exist in the memory of elderly cooks.

This website was created to document these nearly forgotten dishes before they are completely lost. We want to honor the grandmothers and cooks who kept these recipes alive for so long.
