Dinuguan (Tidtad)

Pork Blood Stew Traditional

What is Dinuguan?

Dinuguan is a savory pork blood stew. It is made from pork meat and offal (intestines, liver, heart) cooked in pig's blood, vinegar, garlic, and green chili peppers. The result is a thick, dark, tangy stew that is sometimes called "chocolate meat" because of its color.

In Kapampangan, this dish is called Tidtad, meaning "chopped" — because the pork pieces are finely chopped before cooking.

What Does It Taste Like?

Dinuguan has a strong, savory flavor from the pork blood. The vinegar gives it a tangy taste that balances the richness. Green chili adds a little spice. It is usually eaten with white rice or with puto (steamed rice cake), which soaks up the thick sauce beautifully.

Did You Know? Some Filipinos who are unfamiliar with Dinuguan are hesitant to try it. But once they do, many find it delicious! The blood is fully cooked and thickens the sauce, giving it a unique flavor and texture.

Cultural Significance

Dinuguan is a pre-colonial dish. Before refrigerators existed, using every part of the pig — including the blood — was necessary to avoid waste. It shows the resourcefulness of Kapampangan ancestors who made delicious food from every part of the animal.

Today, Dinuguan is still cooked regularly in Kapampangan homes and is a popular dish at carinderias (small local eateries) throughout Pampanga.

Simple Dinuguan Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 500g pork belly or shoulder, chopped small
  • 200g pork intestines, cleaned and sliced
  • 2 cups pig's blood (fresh or frozen)
  • 1/2 cup white vinegar
  • 1 head garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 green chili peppers (siling haba)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Sauté garlic and onion until soft.
  2. Add pork pieces and cook until lightly browned.
  3. Add pork intestines. Cook for 5 minutes.
  4. Pour in the vinegar. Do not stir for 2 minutes. Then mix.
  5. Add the pig's blood slowly while stirring constantly.
  6. Add chili peppers. Simmer for 15-20 minutes until thick.
  7. Season with salt. Serve hot with rice or puto.
