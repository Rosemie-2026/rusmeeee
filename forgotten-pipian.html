Pipian

Endangered Indigenous Pre-Spanish

What is Pipian?

Pipian is one of the oldest dishes in Kapampangan cuisine. It is believed to predate the Spanish colonization of the Philippines, making it a truly indigenous Filipino dish. It is made from chicken or pork cooked in a thick, nutty sauce made from ground toasted squash seeds (kalabasa seeds), annatto oil, and onion.

The unique thing about Pipian is the use of squash seeds as the thickening agent and main flavor of the sauce. This is very different from Kare-Kare, which uses peanut butter. The squash seed sauce gives Pipian a distinctly earthy, nutty taste that cannot be found in any other dish.

Why Is This Dish So Rare?

Pipian has been almost completely replaced by Kare-Kare. Both dishes are stews with a thick, nutty sauce. But Kare-Kare is more well-known and uses peanut butter, which is cheap and easy to find. Squash seeds must be carefully toasted and ground, which takes much more time and effort.

Over time, cooks started using peanut butter instead of squash seeds, and the original Pipian was forgotten. Today, very few people in Pampanga even know that this dish exists.

⚠️ Status: Endangered. Pipian is one of the most critically endangered dishes in Kapampangan cuisine. The technique of making squash seed sauce has been almost completely forgotten.

The Squash Seed Sauce

The sauce is the heart of Pipian. Here is how it was traditionally made:

  1. Dry squash seeds in the sun for one to two days.
  2. Toast the seeds in a dry pan over low heat until golden brown and fragrant.
  3. Grind the toasted seeds using a stone grinder (gilingan na bato) until very fine.
  4. Mix the ground seeds with hot broth to make a thick paste.
  5. Season with annatto oil, garlic, and onion.

Pipian Recipe (Reconstructed)

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup squash seeds, dried and toasted
  • 2 tablespoons annatto oil (atsuete oil)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • Salt and fish sauce to taste
  • Vegetables: eggplant, string beans (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Toast squash seeds in a dry pan. Grind them finely in a blender.
  2. Sauté garlic and onion in annatto oil until soft.
  3. Add chicken pieces. Cook until lightly browned.
  4. Mix ground squash seeds with chicken broth to make a sauce.
  5. Pour sauce over chicken. Simmer for 20–25 minutes.
  6. Add vegetables and cook until tender.
  7. Season with salt and fish sauce. Serve with rice.

Saving Pipian

Food historians and Kapampangan cultural groups have been working to document the original Pipian recipe. Some chefs have tried to revive it in their restaurants. However, because squash seed sauce is unfamiliar to most diners, it remains extremely rare even in Pampanga.

"Pipian is the dish that proves Kapampangan cuisine existed long before Spain arrived. It is our oldest surviving recipe, and we are losing it." — A Kapampangan food historian
