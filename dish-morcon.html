Morcon

Beef Stuffed Christmas Dish

What is Morcon?

Morcon is a stuffed beef roll that is braised slowly in tomato sauce. A thin slice of beef is spread flat, then layered with hard-boiled eggs, pickles, carrots, chorizo, and cheese. The beef is then rolled up tightly, tied with string, and slow-cooked for hours until tender.

It is a Spanish-influenced dish that became a Kapampangan specialty, especially for Christmas, New Year, and other special occasions.

Morcon Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg beef round, sliced thin and pounded flat
  • 3 hard-boiled eggs, sliced
  • 1 piece chorizo de Bilbao, sliced lengthwise
  • 1 medium carrot, cut into sticks
  • 3 pieces dill pickles
  • 1 can tomato sauce
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • Salt, pepper, soy sauce

Instructions:

  1. Lay the beef flat. Season with salt and pepper.
  2. Layer eggs, chorizo, carrot, and pickles on top.
  3. Roll the beef tightly and tie with kitchen string.
  4. Brown the roll in hot oil on all sides.
  5. Add tomato sauce and beef broth. Simmer for 2 hours.
  6. Slice and serve with the sauce poured on top.

Why Morcon is Important

Morcon represents the Spanish colonial influence on Kapampangan cooking. The technique of stuffing and rolling meat was brought by Spaniards, but Kapampangan cooks made it their own by adding local ingredients and simmering it in their own style.
