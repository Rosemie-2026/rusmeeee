Kare-Kare

Oxtail Peanut Sauce Festive

What is Kare-Kare?

Kare-Kare is a traditional Filipino stew made from oxtail, tripe, and vegetables cooked in a thick, rich peanut sauce. It is one of the most celebrated dishes in Kapampangan cuisine and is always present at special occasions and fiestas.

The dish is always served with bagoong alamang (fermented shrimp paste) on the side. The combination of the mild, rich peanut stew with the salty, pungent shrimp paste is what makes Kare-Kare truly unique.

Origin of the Name

The name "Kare-Kare" is believed to come from the Indian word "kari" (curry), suggesting that the dish may have been influenced by Indian traders who visited the Philippines centuries ago. However, Kapampangan Kare-Kare does not taste like Indian curry at all — it has been completely adapted to local tastes.

Kare-Kare Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg oxtail, cut into pieces
  • 500g banana blossom (puso ng saging)
  • 1 bundle string beans (sitaw)
  • 2 pieces eggplant, sliced
  • 1 cup peanut butter
  • 3 tablespoons annatto seeds (atsuete)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • Salt and fish sauce to taste
  • Bagoong alamang for serving

Instructions:

  1. Boil the oxtail for 2-3 hours until very tender.
  2. Save the broth. Sauté garlic and onion in a large pot.
  3. Dissolve annatto seeds in warm water to get orange color.
  4. Add the broth and annatto water to the pot.
  5. Stir in peanut butter until dissolved into the broth.
  6. Add the oxtail and vegetables. Cook until vegetables are tender.
  7. Season with salt. Serve with bagoong alamang on the side.

Why It Is Important

Kare-Kare is considered a symbol of Filipino hospitality and celebration. A well-made Kare-Kare requires hours of slow cooking and good quality ingredients. It shows the host's dedication to feeding guests well.

"No Kapampangan fiesta is complete without Kare-Kare. It is our most beloved dish."
