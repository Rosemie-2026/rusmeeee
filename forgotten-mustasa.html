Mustasa Adobo (Adobong Mustasa)

Obscure Adobo Variant No Soy Sauce Vegetables

What is Mustasa Adobo?

Mustasa Adobo is a version of the classic Kapampangan white adobo made with mustard greens (mustasa) as the main vegetable. It is cooked without soy sauce, using only white vinegar, garlic, peppercorns, and a small amount of pork or chicken for flavor.

The combination of tangy vinegar and slightly bitter mustard greens creates a dish that is complex, refreshing, and unlike any other adobo in the Philippines. It is very simple to make, but almost no one makes it anymore.

The White Adobo Tradition

Most people think of adobo as a dark, soy-sauce-based dish. But in Pampanga, the original adobo was white — cooked only in vinegar and garlic, with no soy sauce. This is the adobo that existed before soy sauce arrived in the Philippines from Chinese traders.

Mustasa Adobo is a variation of this white adobo tradition. It represents the simplest and most ancient form of Filipino cooking — just vinegar, garlic, and vegetables, slow-cooked until tender.

⚠️ Status: Obscure. Even among Kapampangans, very few people have heard of this dish. It is found only in very old family recipe books and the memories of elderly cooks.

Mustasa Adobo Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 big bundle mustard greens (mustasa), washed and chopped
  • 200g pork belly, sliced thin
  • 1/2 cup white cane vinegar
  • 1 head garlic, crushed
  • 1 teaspoon whole black peppercorns
  • 3 bay leaves
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Render pork belly fat in a pot until slightly crispy.
  2. Add garlic and cook until golden.
  3. Add peppercorns and bay leaves.
  4. Pour in vinegar. Do not stir. Let it boil for 2 minutes.
  5. Add mustard greens. Stir and cook for 5 minutes until wilted.
  6. Season with salt. Serve immediately with hot rice.

Why It Deserves to Be Remembered

Mustasa Adobo may be simple, but it is historically important. It shows what Philippine food tasted like before Spanish colonization. It is healthy, cheap to make, and delicious. If more people knew about it, it might just make a comeback.
